# Framework Figure Selection

Use this guidance when evaluating whether the extracted framework image is actually useful.

## Goal
Pick the single figure that best explains the paper's **method**, **pipeline**, **architecture**, **workflow**, or **overall framework**.

## Prefer
- Figures referenced by captions or nearby text containing words like `pipeline`, `framework`, `architecture`, `workflow`, `overview`, `proposed method`, or `our approach`.
- Figures on pages where the surrounding text looks like the method section.
- Large diagram-like figures with boxes, arrows, modules, stages, or system components.

## Avoid
- First-page teaser figures unless they clearly depict the method.
- Result charts, ablation plots, benchmark tables, qualitative examples, dataset illustrations, or appendix figures.
- Decorative or full-page overview graphics that lack method context.

## Decision rule
If the candidate figure is ambiguous, skip it instead of inserting a misleading image.
